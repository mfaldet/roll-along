# Competitor teardown — Smash Karts

_Cluster: Cosmetics-led competitive (.io). Reads `02-market-frame.md`. Confidence: H/M/L._

**One-liner:** A **small-team** real-time multiplayer kart-battle .io — proof that **fair,
cosmetic-only competitive multiplayer is buildable and runnable without a big studio.**

## Scores (1–5)

| Core | Onbd | Reten | Meta | Econ | Monet | Viral | Social | LiveOps |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4 | 5 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 | 3 |

## How it works (verified)

- **Core (4):** quick real-time kart deathmatch in small arenas (Tall Team, 2020). Web +
  iOS/Android; web distribution via **CrazyGames / Poki**. _(H)_
- **Monetization (3) — strictly fair:** "**no pay-to-win** — cosmetics and battle passes are
  cosmetic only; skill and strategy determine winners." Sells **kart skins, helmets, hats,
  wheels, and celebrations**, with **common→legendary rarity tiers.** _(H)_
- **Team/ops:** a **small team** sustains real-time multiplayer + a cosmetic economy — the
  existence proof Roll Along's multiplayer ambition needs. _(M)_

## Lessons for Roll Along

- ✅ **Borrow — the relevance for the solo constraint.** Smash Karts shows a tiny team *can*
  run fair real-time MP + cosmetics. But note **it's lighter on retention/LiveOps (all 3s)** —
  reinforcing the brief's call to **start async** and layer real-time later. Smash Karts is the
  "yes it's possible" without being the "do it first."
- ✅ **Borrow — rarity tiers on cosmetics.** Common→legendary framing makes the *same* catalogue
  feel deeper and creates aspirational chase items. Roll Along has tiers (starter/standard/
  premium/exclusive) but doesn't surface *rarity as status* — an easy win.
- ✅ **Borrow — "celebrations" as a cosmetic** (third confirmation across the trio: a visible,
  clip-able, low-effort category Roll Along should add).
- ⚠️ **Consider (long-shot) — web distribution as organic UA.** CrazyGames/Poki are real
  discovery channels with zero ad spend — but Roll Along is native SwiftUI; a web build is a
  major lift. Park as a far-future bet, not a near-term move.

## Synthesis from the cosmetics-led competitive cluster

All three monetize the **same way**: **cosmetics that opponents see, in fair competition, kept
fresh by events.** Brawl Stars proves the **fair pass** (8.8×); Stumble proves the **event/skin
treadmill** (and the decline if you stop); Smash Karts proves a **small team can run fair MP**.
**Every one validates Mac's spine** — make identity visible in competition — and every one says
the money follows when looks are *public*, the pass is *cheap and fair*, and the content stays
*fresh*. Roll Along has the catalogue and the social backend; it lacks the **public-cosmetic
surface, the pass, and the event cadence.** That is the roadmap.
