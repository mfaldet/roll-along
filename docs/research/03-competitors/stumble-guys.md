# Competitor teardown — Stumble Guys

_Cluster: Cosmetics-led competitive. Reads `02-market-frame.md`. Confidence: H/M/L._

**One-liner:** A 32-player **physics party-royale** (Fall Guys on mobile) — roller-adjacent
ragdoll chaos, monetized almost entirely on **cosmetics + a pass + a collab/event treadmill.**

## How it works (verified)

- **Core (4):** wobbly physics obstacle races, last-one-standing. Ragdoll fails are
  inherently funny and **clip-able** — the same physics-comedy Roll Along's caroms produce. _(H)_
- **Monetization (4):** skins + emotes + gems/Stumble Tokens, plus the **Stumble Pass**
  (~**$5.99** premium track). Cosmetic-led, no meaningful P2W. _(H)_
- **LiveOps / virality (5) — the engine:** a relentless **creator & brand-collab treadmill**
  — Looney Tunes, TMNT, Ghostbusters, Masters of the Universe — each a limited-time event with
  **exclusive skins** and urgency. **Mythic skins are tied to each season's final tournament**,
  fusing the cosmetic chase to competition. _(H)_
- **Scale & caution:** **250M+ downloads**, revenue **peaked ~$70M then declined** — even a
  strong cosmetic-led game **fades without sustained novelty.** The collab/event treadmill *is*
  the retention engine, not a bonus. _(M)_

## Lessons for Roll Along

- ✅ **Borrow — exclusive *event* skins create the urgency a fair game otherwise lacks.** Roll
  Along already half-has this (seasonal bundles, Challenge-Track reward bundles) — the missing
  piece is a **recurring, time-boxed event cadence** that drops limited cosmetics (no FOMO
  guilt — framed as "this season's look," cleanly within guardrails).
- ✅ **Borrow — tie cosmetics to competition.** Stumble's Mythic-per-tournament pattern → Roll
  Along: a **seasonal competitive ladder whose top cosmetic can only be earned by placing.**
  Pure flex, no P2W, maximal visibility. Marries the social/competitive/cosmetic spine.
- ✅ **Borrow — emotes/celebrations** as a cosmetic category (cross-confirmed with Brawl/Smash).
- ⚠️ **Adapt — creator collabs at indie scale.** Brand licenses are out of reach solo; but
  **small-creator collabs** (a TikTok creator co-designs a skin, promotes it to their audience)
  are indie-viable and double as **organic UA** — directly serves the no-paid-UA constraint.
- ⚠️ **Heed the caution — novelty is the retention tax.** A fair cosmetic game *must* keep
  shipping fresh looks/events or it declines. This raises the priority of a **cheap, repeatable
  content/event pipeline** (remote-config + a skin-drop calendar), not one-off bundles.
