# 02 — Market Frame & Analysis Rubric

_Artifact 2. Reads `00-brief.md` + `01-roll-along-teardown.md`. Locks positioning, the
competitor set, and the rubric the teardowns (Prompt 3) follow._

---

## Positioning (locked)

**Hybrid-casual physics roller with a competitive/social spine and a deep cosmetic meta.**

North star: a **fair, social, cosmetic-led competitive marble platform** — closest in
monetization *philosophy* to Brawl Stars / Stumble Guys / Fortnite, built on a tilt-roller
core. We are **not** chasing hyper-casual ad-arbitrage scale, nor midcore whale extraction.

## The spine the research must illuminate (confirmed design principle)

**Make a player's identity & skill visible to others.** Concretely, starting in competitive
modes: opponents render **their own ball skin + trail** (PvP-visible identity → status/desire);
**floor/background + goal render the viewer's own** cosmetic (personal world). Each competitor
teardown's #1 job: **how does this app turn cosmetic visibility + social + events into
retention and *fair* revenue?** That mechanism is the transferable gold.

## Rubric — score each app 1–5 per axis + extract mechanics

Nine axes (same as the teardown): core loop · onboarding/D1 · retention · meta-progression ·
economy · monetization · virality · social · LiveOps/events.

**1–5 scale:** 1 absent/weak · 2 basic · 3 competent · 4 strong · 5 best-in-class/defining.

Per app capture, tightly: **core loop**, **retention hooks**, **monetization model**
(IAP / ads / **battle pass** / sub), **LiveOps/event cadence**, **virality & UA**, **ASO**,
**price points** — each with a **source** and **confidence (H/M/L)**.

> The output is **not** a wiki. For every notable mechanic, answer: *"Could Roll Along borrow
> this, and what's the evidence it works?"* — flagged ✅ borrow / ⚠️ adapt / ⛔ off-limits
> (against the brief's guardrails: no forced ads, no loot boxes, no P2W, no predatory FOMO).

## Method & honesty

Grounded in model knowledge (cutoff ~Jan 2026) **plus targeted web verification** for
specifics that move fast — current battle-pass prices, recent monetization/LiveOps changes,
scale/revenue. Revenue and download figures are **directional and cited**; precise
Sensor-Tower-grade data is paywalled. Confidence tagged H/M/L throughout.
